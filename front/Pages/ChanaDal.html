Chana Dal

Chana Dal, also known as split chickpeas, is a staple ingredient in Indian cuisine and is widely consumed around the world due to its nutritional benefits and versatile culinary applications. Chana Dal is derived from black chickpeas (kala chana) that are split and husked to produce small, yellow-colored lentils. These lentils are rich in protein, dietary fiber, vitamins, and minerals, making them a valuable addition to a balanced diet.

In terms of taste and texture, Chana Dal offers a nutty flavor and a firm texture when cooked, making it suitable for various dishes. It can be boiled and seasoned as a standalone dish or used as an ingredient in soups, stews, curries, salads, and snacks.
